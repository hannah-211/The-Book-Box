Top 10 Fantasy Books

The Fellowship of the Ring - J.R.R. Tolkien

In a sleepy village in the Shire, a young hobbit is entrusted with an immense task. He must make a perilous journey across Middle-Earth to the Cracks of Doom, there to destroy the Ruling Ring of Power - the only thing that prevents the Dark Lord's evil dominion.

Harry Potter and the Philsopher's Stone - J.K. Rowling

Harry Potter had never even heard of Hogwarts when the letters start dropping on the doormat at number four, Privet Drive. Then, on Harry's eleventh birthday, a great beetle-eyed giant of a man bursts in with some astonishing news, Harry Potter is a wizard.

Good Omens - Terry Pratchett & Neil Gaiman

People have been predicting the end of the world almost from its very beginning, so it's only natural to be sceptical when a new date is set for Judgement Day. this time though, the armies of Good and Evil really do appear to be massing. But one fast-living demon and a somewhat fussy angel would quite like the Rapture not to happen.

The Bear and the Nightingale - Katherine Arden

In a village at the edge of the wilderness of northern Russia, an elderly servant tells stories of sorcery, folklore, and the Winter King. But for the young, wild Vasya these are far more than just stories. She alone can sense the growing forces of dark magic in the woods...

The Princess Bride - William Goldman

When the beautiful Buttercup learns that her only love Westley's ship has been captured by the terrible Dread Pirate Roberts, she is devastated. Buttercup is kidnapped by a trio of masked men, pursued by mysterious masked man in black. Who is this individual, and what may tie him to Buttercup's heartbreak?

A Darker Shade of Magic - V.E. Schwab

Most people only know one London; but what if there were several? Kell is one of the last Travelers - magicians with a rare ability to travel between parallel Londons. There's Grey London, Red London, and White London. But once upon a time, there was Black London...

Empire of Sand - Tasha Suri

Mehr is a girl trapped between two cultures. Her mother's people were outcasts, Amrithi nomads who worshipped he spirits of the sands. Caugh performing these forbidden rites, Mehr is brought to the attention of the Emperor's most feared mystics. If she fails in their bidding, the gods themselves may awaken.

The Eye of the World - Robert Jordan

The Wheel of Time turns and Ages come and pass, leaving memories that become legend. Legend fades to myth and even myth is long forgotten when the Age that gave it birth returns again. But one truth remains, and what mortal men forget, the Aes Sedai do not...

Whispers Underground - Ben Aaronovitch

Peter Grant is learning magic fast. And it's just as well - he's already had run-ins with the deadly supernatural children of the Thames. Progression in the Police Force is less easy. Especially when you work in a department that doesn't even officially exist. Something horrible is happening in the labyrinth of tunnels that make up the tube of London. Time to call in PC Peter Grant, Britain's Last Wizard.

Caraval - Stephanie Garber

Caraval is magic, mystery adventure and for Scarlett and her beloved sister Tella it represents freedom and an escape from their ruthless, abusive father. When Scarlett discovers her father has arranged a marriage for her she believes all her hopes of escape have been dashed. No sooner have they entered the confines of Caraval than Tella vanishes...
